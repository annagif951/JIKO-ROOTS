Why we cook this way

"My grandmother never owned an oven. She owned a jiko, a box of charcoal, and forty years of knowing exactly when the meat was ready by smell alone." — Auma Otieno, founder

Auma opened Jiko Roots in 2016 after ten years cooking in restaurant kitchens that ran on gas lines and timers. She missed the unpredictability — the way charcoal runs hotter on one side, the way you have to stand near the fire to read it.

So the kitchen here has no gas connection at all. Everything that reaches your table has been near a flame that someone was watching. It's slower. It's also why regulars order the same three dishes for years and still ask what's different tonight.
